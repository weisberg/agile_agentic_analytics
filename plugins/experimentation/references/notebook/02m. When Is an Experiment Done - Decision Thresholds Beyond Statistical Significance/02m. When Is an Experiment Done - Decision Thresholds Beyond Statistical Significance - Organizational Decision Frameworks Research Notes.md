# Organizational Decision Frameworks Research Notes

## Source 1: GrowthBook Experiment Decision Framework (EDF)

### Overview
The Experiment Decision Framework is a set of tools and customizable settings to help streamline and improve experiment decision making. Answers questions: "Should I keep running my experiment?" and "Do my results meet my success criteria?"

### Two Key Components

**1. Decision Criteria**
How results lead to decisions. Two out-of-the-box options:
- **Clear Signals**: Requires no guardrail failures AND all goal metric successes
- **Do No Harm**: Only requires no metrics are statistically significant in harmful direction

**2. Target Minimum Detectable Effects (MDEs)**
Smallest effects you want to reliably detect before making a decision.
- GrowthBook default: 10%
- Example: If confidence intervals range 0-20%, then 10% lift is significant and target MDE reached

### Setup Requirements

**Minimum experiment runtime:**
- Default: 3 days
- Larger values (7 days) reasonable for strong day-of-week effects
- Prevents premature decisions during early data collection

**Target MDE selection:**
- Balance: lift profitability vs experiment runtime
- Small target MDEs: detect tinier effects, require more data
- Large target MDEs: shorter runtimes, but may face ambiguity if true effects smaller

**Goal metric recommendations:**
- Ideally only 1-2 goal metrics
- 3+ goal metrics significantly extends experiment duration
- Use secondary metrics for deep dives, not decision criteria

### Experiment Statuses and Actions

| Status | Conditions | Action |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Unhealthy | Imbalanced traffic (SRM), multiple exposures, low-powered | Troubleshoot or see FAQ |
| No data | No users in experiment after refresh | Check traffic, feature flags, data configuration |
| ~X days left | Not reached target power | Continue collecting data or adjust MDEs/goal metrics |
| Ship now | Decision criteria met + (Sequential testing enabled OR target MDEs reached OR strict significance p<0.001/CTW>99.9%) | Ship the variation |
| Roll back now | Rollback criteria met for all variations + same conditions | Stop and keep status quo |
| Ready for review | Review criteria met + same conditions | Consider trade-offs: ship, rollback, iterate, or continue |

### Decision Logic

**Three paths to decision:**
1. Sequential testing enabled (allows continuous monitoring)
2. Goal metrics reached target MDEs (desired precision achieved)
3. Effects significant at strict threshold (Frequentist p<0.001; Bayesian CTW>99.9%)

**Key insight:** Can make decision even if not statistically significant at traditional levels, if target MDE reached and comfortable with that level of uncertainty.

### Power and Precision

**Target power definition:**
Enough data to reliably detect target MDE for all goal metrics. More data = more precision, but target power = sufficient for decision.

**Low power troubleshooting:**
- Target MDE too low for traffic/metric (especially <1% conversion rates)
- Too many goal metrics (splits focus, increases required power)
- Too many variations (splits traffic, lowers power)

**Recommendation:** Evaluate comfort with uncertainty, consider increasing target MDE

## Source 2: GoodUI - Practical Stopping Criteria (May 2021)
Author: Jakub Linowski

### The Messy Reality

Traditional approach: Fixed duration or dynamic stop rule (significance threshold)
Reality: Multiple factors influence stopping decisions in practice

### Five Practical Stopping Criteria

**1. Minimum Duration**
- Predefined minimum (1+ weeks typical)
- Reasons:
  - Adequate sample size for statistical power
  - Representative time frame (covers business cycle/cohort)
  - Protection from early noise/chance effects
- **Decision rule:** If minimum duration reached → more likely to stop than not

**2. Significance Level**
- Strong effects pressure early stopping (especially negative)
- **Practical threshold:** Not just p<0.05 on day 2
- **Early stop justified:** Strong negative effect with multiple coherent metrics
- Real example: Stopped experiment early due to strong negative effect across multiple metrics
- **Key insight:** Wouldn't stop for marginal p=0.05 on day 2, but would stop for strong coherent negative signal

**3. Backlog Pressure**
- Prebuilt tests waiting in queue → pressure to move on
- No tests in queue → let tests run longer
- **Decision rule:** Better to let insignificant test run longer than have no tests running
- Sometimes insignificant = underestimated sample/duration, not truly no effect
- Extra exposure may provide clearer signal

**4. Coherency Across Metrics**
- Multiple metrics show if test is coherent or noisy
- **Example (e-commerce funnel):**
  - Coherent: Product page improvement → positive through all funnel steps → sales
  - Incoherent: Metrics all over the place → sign of chance/noise
- **Decision rule:** 
  - Coherent metrics → comfortable stopping (even if primary metric not significant but positive, with initial metrics significant and positive)
  - Incoherent metrics → needs more time (even if primary metric significant)

**5. Consistency With Prior Data**
- Similar experiments with similar results → stronger signal, greater confidence
- Similar experiments with opposing effects → sign of incomplete experiment
- **Exception:** When opposing effects equally trustworthy, powered, and highly significant → accept as is

### Visual Decision Framework

**More likely to KEEP RUNNING:**
- Minimum duration NOT reached
- LOW significance
- NO other tests in backlog
- INCOHERENT metrics
- INCONSISTENT with prior data

**More likely to STOP:**
- Minimum duration REACHED
- HIGH significance
- HAVE other tests waiting
- COHERENT metrics
- CONSISTENT with prior data

### Future Direction
Author advocates for automating these stopping rules as explicit algorithm. Code could stop and decide on experiments more optimally than humans.

## Key Organizational Insights

### Decision Latency vs Statistical Certainty Trade-offs

**GrowthBook approach:**
- Formalize the trade-off through target MDEs
- Organizations set acceptable precision levels
- Can decide before traditional significance if target precision reached
- Makes uncertainty explicit and acceptable

**GoodUI approach:**
- Acknowledge multiple pressures beyond statistics
- Business context (backlog, urgency) influences decisions
- Coherency and consistency provide additional confidence signals
- Practical stopping = weighted combination of factors

### Standardization vs Flexibility

**GrowthBook (standardized):**
- Organization-wide defaults for MDEs and decision criteria
- Customizable per experiment
- Clear status badges and recommendations
- Reduces decision paralysis

**GoodUI (flexible):**
- Human judgment weighs multiple factors
- Context-dependent decisions
- Acknowledges messiness of real-world practice
- Advocates eventual automation

### Risk Management Approaches

**Explicit risk quantification (GrowthBook):**
- Target MDE = acceptable uncertainty level
- Decision criteria = risk tolerance (Clear Signals vs Do No Harm)
- Strict thresholds for early stopping (p<0.001)

**Implicit risk management (GoodUI):**
- Minimum duration protects from early noise
- Coherency across metrics validates signal
- Consistency with history reduces risk
- Strong negative effects trigger early stops

### Practical Implementation Challenges

**Common issues:**
1. Too many goal metrics (extends duration, reduces power)
2. Target MDE too small for traffic (low power, long duration)
3. Too many variations (splits traffic, reduces power)
4. Competing goal metrics (ambiguous decisions)

**Solutions:**
1. Limit to 1-2 goal metrics for decisions
2. Set realistic MDEs based on traffic and business impact
3. Use secondary metrics for understanding, not decisions
4. Pre-commit to decision criteria before experiment starts

### Organizational Maturity Indicators

**Less mature:**
- Ad-hoc stopping decisions
- Peeking without controls
- No standardized criteria
- Individual judgment varies widely

**More mature:**
- Formalized decision frameworks
- Pre-committed stopping rules
- Standardized MDEs and criteria
- Automated recommendations
- Clear documentation and transparency
