## Overview

The Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) regulates financial promotions in the United
Kingdom under Section 21 of the Financial Services and Markets Act 2000 (FSMA). A
"financial promotion" is an invitation or inducement to engage in investment activity
communicated in the course of business.

## The Clear, Fair, and Not Misleading Standard

Under FCA Principle 7 and COBS 4.2, all financial promotions must be clear, fair,
and not misleading.

### Clear

- Key information must be prominent and easily understandable to the target audience.
- Font size, placement, and formatting must ensure readability.
- Technical jargon must be explained or avoided in retail communications.
- Important limitations, conditions, and qualifications must not be hidden in
  small print or footnotes.
- Information architecture must lead with material facts, not marketing claims.

### Fair

- Benefits and risks must receive balanced treatment.
- The promotion must not emphasize potential benefits without giving a fair and
  prominent indication of relevant risks.
- Cherry-picking favorable data or time periods is prohibited.
- Comparisons with other products must be meaningful, fair, and presented in a
  balanced way.
- Any comparison must use the same basis and time periods.

### Not Misleading

- The promotion must not create a false or deceptive impression, whether by content,
  design, presentation, or omission.
- It must not disguise, diminish, or obscure important information, statements, or
  warnings.
- The overall impression must be consistent with the detailed content.
- Headlines and prominent claims must be supported by the body text.
- Images and design elements must not create misleading impressions.

## Risk Warning Requirements

### General Risk Warning

All promotions for investments where capital is at risk must include:
- A clear statement that capital is at risk.
- The potential for loss of some or all of the investment.

### Product-Specific Risk Warnings

**UCITS and Retail AIFs:**
- "The value of investments and the income from them can go down as well as up and
  you may not get back the amount originally invested."

**Non-UCITS Retail Schemes:**
- Enhanced risk warning reflecting illiquidity and complexity.

**Structured Deposits:**
- Warning that returns depend on the performance of the underlying asset.
- Credit risk of the deposit taker.

**CFDs and Spread Bets:**
- Percentage of retail investor accounts that lose money (firm-specific figure).
- Warning about rapid loss potential due to leverage.

### Past Performance Warning

When past performance is shown:
- "Past performance is not a reliable indicator of future results."
- Must be presented prominently (not buried in footnotes).
- Performance data must cover at least 5 years (or the full period if shorter).
- Data must be based on complete 12-month periods.
- The reference currency must be clearly stated.
- Gross vs. net basis must be clearly identified.

### High-Risk Investment Warnings (PS22/10)

For high-risk investments (as classified by the FCA), additional requirements apply:
- Prominent risk warning on the first page or screen.
- Personalized risk warning pop-up for digital promotions.
- Clear statement: "Don't invest unless you're prepared to lose all the money you
  invest."
- Cooling-off period requirements for certain high-risk investments.
- Ban on incentives to invest (e.g., refer-a-friend bonuses for restricted mass
  market investments).

## Target Market Assessment

### Retail Investors

- Language must be understandable by a typical retail investor without specialist
  knowledge.
- Complexity must be appropriate to the target market.
- Risk level must be clearly communicated.
- Clear identification of whether the product is appropriate for the investor type.

### Professional Investors

- May use more technical language and assume greater financial literacy.
- Risk warnings still required but may be less prescriptive.
- Reduced formatting requirements compared to retail promotions.

### Eligible Counterparties

- Minimal promotional restrictions.
- Standard business communications principles apply.

## Digital Promotions and Social Media

### General Requirements

- Character-limited promotions (e.g., social media) must still meet the clear, fair,
  and not misleading standard.
- Where space is limited, risk warnings must be immediately accessible (e.g., one
  click away).
- Standalone compliance: each post or ad must be compliant on its own, not dependent
  on other content for context.

### Influencer and Affiliate Promotions

- Any person communicating a financial promotion must be authorized or the promotion
  must be approved by an authorized firm.
- The relationship between the influencer and the firm must be disclosed.
- The authorized firm retains responsibility for compliance.

## Approval and Record-Keeping

### Approval Process

- Financial promotions must be approved by an authorized person before publication.
- The approving firm must have the competence and expertise to approve the specific
  type of promotion.
- The FCA gateway for financial promotion approval limits which firms can approve
  third-party promotions.

### Record-Keeping Requirements

- Firms must retain financial promotions for a minimum of three years from the date
  the promotion was last communicated.
- Records must include the final version as communicated and any supporting compliance
  review documentation.

## Scanner Keywords and Patterns

**High Severity:**
- "guaranteed," "risk-free," "safe," "secure returns," "certain profit"
- Capital-at-risk products without risk warnings
- Missing past performance disclaimers

**Medium Severity:**
- Benefit claims without balancing risk information
- Past performance data not covering minimum 5-year period
- Missing currency denomination
- Non-prominent risk warnings (e.g., small font, footnote only)

**Low Severity:**
- Technical jargon in retail-facing content
- Comparisons without common basis
- Forward-looking statements without qualification
