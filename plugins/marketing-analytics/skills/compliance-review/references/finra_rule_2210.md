## Overview

FINRA Rule 2210 establishes standards for broker-dealer communications with the
public. It defines three categories of communications, each with different approval,
supervision, and filing requirements.

## Communication Categories

### Retail Communication
Any written (including electronic) communication distributed or made available to
more than 25 retail investors within any 30 calendar-day period.

**Requirements:**
- Must be approved by a registered principal prior to first use.
- Subject to FINRA filing requirements (see Filing Requirements below).
- Must be fair and balanced.

### Institutional Communication
Any written (including electronic) communication distributed or made available only
to institutional investors. "Institutional investor" includes persons with total
assets of at least $50 million or entities listed in Rule 2210(a)(4).

**Requirements:**
- Must be supervised by the member firm (policies and procedures).
- Not required to be pre-approved by a principal before use.
- Subject to post-use filing within 10 business days for certain content.

### Correspondence
Any written (including electronic) communication distributed or made available to
25 or fewer retail investors within any 30 calendar-day period.

**Requirements:**
- Subject to supervision policies (spot-check review).
- Not required to be pre-approved by a principal before use.
- Not subject to FINRA filing requirements.

## Content Standards

### Fair and Balanced (Rule 2210(d)(1))

All communications must:
- Be based on principles of fair dealing and good faith.
- Be fair and balanced.
- Provide a sound basis for evaluating the facts.
- Not omit any material fact or qualification that would cause the communication
  to be misleading.

### Prohibited Content

Communications must NOT:
- Make any false, exaggerated, unwarranted, promissory, or misleading statement
  or claim.
- Publish or distribute opinions or recommendations without a reasonable basis.
- Predict or project performance, imply that past performance will recur, or make
  any exaggerated or unwarranted claim about investment results.
- State or imply that returns are guaranteed.
- Use the terms "safe," "guaranteed," "risk-free," or similar terms suggesting
  absence of risk when referring to securities.

### Risk and Benefit Balance

- Any statement about benefits must be balanced with a discussion of risks or
  limitations.
- Risk factors must receive comparable prominence to benefits.
- Risks cannot be relegated to footnotes when benefits are featured prominently.

### Specific Product Requirements

**Options:** Must include the standardized options disclosure document (ODD) or
information on how to obtain it.

**Mutual Funds and Variable Products:** Must disclose sales charges, expenses, and
that investment returns and principal value fluctuate.

**Leveraged and Inverse ETFs:** Must explain that these products reset daily and
may perform differently than expected over longer periods.

**Structured Products:** Must clearly explain key features, risks, and the potential
for loss of principal.

## Performance Presentation

### General Requirements
- Historical performance must include relevant time periods.
- Performance must be current through the most recent calendar quarter.
- All fees and charges must be reflected or disclosed.
- Standardized performance presentations are required for mutual funds (SEC Rule
  482 / Rule 34b-1).

### Prohibited Practices
- Cannot project future performance.
- Cannot imply past performance will recur.
- Cannot present performance in a misleading manner.
- Cannot use non-standard time periods to highlight favorable results.

## Filing Requirements

### Pre-Use Filing (10 Business Days Before First Use)

Required for:
- **New members** -- All retail communications during the first year of FINRA
  membership.
- **Options** -- Retail communications concerning options (filed with OCC).
- **CMOs** -- Retail communications concerning collateralized mortgage obligations.
- **Registered investment companies** -- Certain communications regarding mutual funds,
  ETFs, and variable products.

### Post-Use Filing (Within 10 Business Days of First Use)

Required for:
- Retail communications concerning registered investment companies that are not
  subject to pre-use filing.
- Institutional communications concerning registered investment companies.
- Any communication for which FINRA has imposed a post-use filing requirement
  pursuant to Rule 2210(c)(4).

### Exempt from Filing

- Correspondence (25 or fewer retail investors in 30 days).
- Communications previously filed and not materially altered.
- Certain tombstone advertisements and prospectuses.

## Supervision Requirements

### Principal Approval
- Retail communications: pre-approval by registered principal required.
- Institutional communications: review and supervision per firm policies.
- Correspondence: spot-check supervision per firm policies.

### Recordkeeping
- Firms must retain copies of all communications for at least three years from
  the date of last use.
- Filing records must be maintained with proof of filing date.
- Review and approval records must be maintained.

## Scanner Keywords and Patterns

The following patterns trigger FINRA-related compliance flags:

**High Severity (Definite Violations):**
- "guaranteed return," "risk-free," "safe investment," "no risk"
- "will earn," "will achieve," "certain to"
- Projection language: "will increase," "expected to return [specific %]"

**Medium Severity (Likely Issues):**
- Benefit statements without corresponding risk disclosure
- Performance claims without time period specification
- Comparison to benchmarks without proper identification

**Low Severity (Potential Concerns):**
- Use of superlatives: "best," "top," "leading," "premier"
- Forward-looking statements without appropriate qualifying language
- References to past performance without standard disclaimers
